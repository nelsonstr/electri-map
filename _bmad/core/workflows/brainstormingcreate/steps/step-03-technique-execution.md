# Step 3: Technique Execution

## Goal

Execute the selected brainstorming technique(s) to generate innovative ideas for emergency services improvement.

## Input

- Session setup summary from Step 1
- Selected technique(s) from Step 2
- Emergency brainstorming techniques from `{installed_path}/emergency-methods.csv`

## Process

### 3.1 Warm-Up (5-10 minutes)

Start with a quick warm-up to get creative juices flowing:

**Emergency Services Warm-Up Prompts:**
1. "Describe the perfect emergency response that saves everyone with zero risk."
2. "Imagine citizens are data - how would you optimize emergency 'delivery' to them?"
3. "What would a 5-star emergency service look like on Yelp?"
4. "If emergency alerts were ads, what would they say?"

### 3.2 Core Ideation Session

Execute the primary technique with emergency services adaptations:

#### For SCAMPER:
Guide the user through each SCAMPER element with emergency-specific prompts:

**Substitute:**
- What if we substitute human responders with robots in dangerous situations?
- What if we substitute diesel with electric in fleet vehicles?
- What if we substitute central dispatch with distributed decision-making?
- What if we substitute professional responders with trained volunteers for certain calls?

**Combine:**
- How can we combine fire and medical services more effectively?
- What resources can be combined across jurisdictions?
- How can we combine prevention and response in one service?
- What data sources can be combined for better situational awareness?

**Adapt:**
- How have other countries solved similar challenges?
- What best practices from other industries can be adapted?
- What worked in past emergencies that we can adapt for current needs?
- How can we adapt technology from other sectors for emergency use?

**Modify:**
- How can we modify response protocols for faster action?
- What can be scaled up or down based on demand?
- How can we modify communication for better public understanding?
- What can be pre-positioned or pre-planned?

**Put to another use:**
- How can current equipment be repurposed?
- What existing infrastructure can serve emergency purposes?
- How can volunteer networks be put to new uses?
- What data collected for one purpose can serve another?

**Eliminate:**
- What steps in current processes are unnecessary?
- What bureaucratic hurdles can be eliminated?
- What duplicated efforts can be eliminated?
- What can be automated to eliminate human error?

**Reverse:**
- What if citizens responded to us instead of us to them?
- What if we paid for prevention instead of paying for response?
- What if we trained citizens to be first responders?
- What if emergencies were reported by sensors instead of humans?

#### For 5 Whys:
Guide the user through root cause analysis:

1. "What problem are we trying to solve?"
2. "Why does this problem exist?"
3. "Why [deeper reason]?"
4. "Why [even deeper]?"
5. "Why [root cause]?"

**Emergency Services Example:**
- Problem: High response times in rural areas
- Why? Long distances to travel
- Why? Few stations in rural areas
- Why? Budget constraints limit station construction
- Why? Population density doesn't justify investment
- Why? Emergency services are funded reactively rather than preventively

#### For Empathy Mapping:
Create empathy maps for different stakeholders:

**Citizens in Emergency:**
- **Think & Feel**: "Will my family be safe?" "Is this really happening?"
- **Hear**: Emergency sirens, TV alerts, neighbor's warnings
- **See**: Flashing lights, smoke, chaos, emergency personnel
- **Say & Do**: Call 112, try to evacuate, wait for instructions
- **Pain**: Fear, uncertainty, being helpless, property loss
- **Gain**: Safety, information, help, reassurance

**Emergency Responders:**
- **Think & Feel**: "Can I save everyone?" "Am I making the right call?"
- **Hear**: Radio communications, dispatch, commands
- **See**: Victims, hazards, conditions changing rapidly
- **Say & Do**: Take action, communicate, coordinate, make decisions
- **Pain**: Pressure, trauma, responsibility, fatigue
- **Gain**: Purpose, helping others, teamwork, mastery

### 3.3 Idea Documentation

Record all ideas with:

- **Idea Number**: Sequential identifier
- **Category**: Which SCAMPER element or theme
- **Description**: Brief description of the idea
- **Impact Potential**: High/Medium/Low
- **Feasibility**: Easy/Medium/Hard
- **Risk Level**: Low/Medium/High
- **Implementation Notes**: Brief thoughts on how to implement

### 3.4 Continue Until Goals Met

Continue ideation until:
- Target idea count reached (minimum 50 for breakthrough thinking)
- All categories explored
- User indicates readiness to organize

### 3.5 Domain Shifts (Anti-Bias Protocol)

Every 10 ideas, consciously shift creative domain:

**Domains to Rotate Through:**
1. **Operational**: How we do things
2. **Technological**: Tools and systems
3. **Human**: Personnel and training
4. **Community**: Public-facing
5. **Financial**: Budget and resources
6. **Regulatory**: Compliance and policy
7. **Prevention**: Risk reduction
8. **Partnerships**: Collaborations
